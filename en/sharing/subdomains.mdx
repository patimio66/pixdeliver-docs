## Overview

A subdomain is your personal space in PixDeliver. Once it is set up, all of your galleries and selections are available at a professional-looking address `yourstudio.pixdeliver.com`, instead of the basic `pixdeliver.com/g/gallery-name`.

**Key information:**

- The subdomain is assigned to **your account**, not to a single gallery — one subdomain covers all of your galleries and selections
- The feature is **free** and available on every plan (even without an active Studio plan)
- The subdomain is **optional** — without it, galleries still work at the basic address `pixdeliver.com/g/...`
- It does not require DNS configuration on your end — we handle everything automatically

<Info>
  Do you have the **Studio plan**? You can go a step further and share galleries under your **custom domain** (e.g. `gallery.yourstudio.com`). The custom domain then takes priority, while the subdomain remains active as a backup address. See: [Custom domain](/en/sharing/custom-domain).
</Info>

## How to set up a subdomain

**Instructions:**

1. Go to **Settings** → **Profile**
2. In the **Subdomain (optional)** field, enter your chosen name
3. The system checks availability in real time — you will see a message:
   - "Checking availability..."
   - ✓ "This subdomain is available"
   - ✗ "The subdomain is taken, reserved, or has an invalid format"
4. Click **Save**

**Result:** The subdomain works right away. All of your galleries are available at the new address.

### Name requirements

<Info>
  - **Length:** from 3 to 63 characters
  - **Allowed characters:** lowercase letters (`a-z`), digits (`0-9`), hyphens (`-`)
  - **Cannot** start or end with a hyphen
  - **Must be unique** across the entire system
  - **Reserved words are blocked** — including `www`, `api`, `admin`, `mail`, `app`, `cdn`, `dev`, `test`, `staging`, `dashboard`, `support`, `blog`, `shop`, and dozens of other technical prefixes
</Info>

<Tip>
  If your favorite name is taken, add an initial or a specialty — e.g. `kowalski-foto`, `jk-studio`, `kowalski-wesela`.
</Tip>

## URLs after setting up a subdomain

Once you have a subdomain set up (e.g. `kowalski`), your galleries and selections are available at short addresses:

| Resource | Address |
| --- | --- |
| Public gallery | `https://kowalski.pixdeliver.com/wesele-anna-tomasz` |
| Photo selection | `https://kowalski.pixdeliver.com/select/{token}` |
| Single photo download | handled automatically from within the gallery |

Without a subdomain, the same resources are available at the basic addresses:

| Resource | Address |
| --- | --- |
| Public gallery | `https://pixdeliver.com/g/wesele-anna-tomasz` |
| Photo selection | `https://pixdeliver.com/select/{token}` |

<Info>
  After setting up a subdomain, **both formats** usually still work, but it is recommended to share only the subdomain version — it looks more professional and builds brand recognition.
</Info>

## Changing the subdomain

You can change the subdomain name at any time in **Settings** → **Profile**.

<Warning>
  **Old links stop working immediately.** After changing the subdomain, all previously shared addresses with the old name will return an error. The system will show a modal warning you before saving the change if there are galleries on the account.
</Warning>

**After changing the subdomain you must:**

- Copy the new gallery links from the management panel
- Send the updated links to clients who have not yet downloaded their photos
- Update the links on your business website, in emails, and on business cards

<Tip>
  It is best to set up the subdomain **before** sharing your first gallery and not change it afterward.
</Tip>

## Removing the subdomain

If you want to return to the basic `pixdeliver.com/g/...` addresses:

1. Go to **Settings** → **Profile**
2. **Clear** the subdomain field (leave it empty)
3. Save

**Result:** Your galleries go back to the basic addresses. The subdomain becomes available for other users.

<Warning>
  The system will block removal of the subdomain if it would create a gallery slug conflict — that is, when your gallery has the same slug as another user's gallery served from the main domain. In that case, first change the slug of the conflicting gallery.
</Warning>

## Access limits

Subdomains have the same anti-abuse protections as the basic addresses:

| Action | Limit |
| --- | --- |
| Entering a gallery password | 10 attempts / minute / IP |
| Downloading a single photo | 60 / minute / IP |
| Generating a download link | 5 / minute / IP |
| Opening a selection | 60 / minute / IP |

The limits apply to public access and are invisible to a typical user — they protect galleries from automated downloading.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can I use my own domain (e.g. `foto.moja-strona.pl`)?**
Yes — as part of the **Studio plan**. You can connect your own domain instead of a subdomain; you will find the configuration details in the [Custom domain](/en/sharing/custom-domain) guide. On the other plans, the free `*.pixdeliver.com` subdomain is available.

**Does the subdomain cost anything?**
No. The feature is free and available on every plan — even if you do not have any active paid gallery.

**Do I need to configure DNS or buy a domain?**
No. We handle everything automatically on the PixDeliver side.

**Can each gallery have its own separate subdomain?**
No. The subdomain is shared across the entire account — it covers all of your galleries and selections.

**What happens when someone releases an attractive subdomain?**
Subdomains work on a first-come, first-served basis. There is no queue and no notifications about names being released.

**Does the basic address `pixdeliver.com/g/{slug}` stop working after setting up a subdomain?**
The basic address still works. However, we recommend sharing only the subdomain version.
